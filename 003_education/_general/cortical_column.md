A cortical column is the **basic functional unit of the [neocortex](neocortex.md),** first described by Vernon Mountcastle in 1957. Each column consists of ~100–300 neurons arranged vertically across all six cortical layers, sharing consistent connectivity and a common receptive field. The image above shows five columns in rat vibrissal (whisker) cortex, with Layer 4 (yellow) serving as the primary thalamic input layer.
